Workday's SCIM connector is not turnkey — Workday does not ship a
standard SCIM v2.0 connector. Integration requires Workday Studio,
a mid-tier connector, or fronting Workday through an IdP (Okta /
Entra ID) and SCIM'ing from there. Reference:
[ory/integrates/directory-sync/workday-scim](https://github.com/ory/integrates/tree/main/directory-sync/workday-scim).
</Admonition>

[Workday](https://www.workday.com) is the most widely deployed
enterprise HCM and the source of truth for employee data in many
enterprises. Wiring Workday → Ory keeps Ory's identity store in
sync with HR ground truth.

## Integration patterns

Pick one of three:

**Pattern A — Workday Studio integration system** (canonical but
expensive). Build a Workday integration that maps Workday data
sources (Worker, Position, etc.) into SCIM 2.0 calls against Ory's
directory-sync endpoint. Requires Workday Studio licensing and
developer hours.

**Pattern B — mid-tier connector**. Deploy a connector (Tools4ever
HelloID, Saviynt, or a custom service) that pulls Workday REST/SOAP
and pushes to Ory's SCIM endpoint. Lower friction than Studio.

**Pattern C — provision through your IdP** (the most common pattern in production deployments).
Provision Workday → Okta/Entra via the IdP's native Workday
connector, then provision IdP → Ory via the existing
[Okta SCIM](../../polis/directory-sync/providers/okta.mdx) or [Microsoft Entra SCIM](../../polis/directory-sync/providers/azure.mdx)
integration. Avoids touching Workday directly.

## What Ory provides

A standard SCIM 2.0 endpoint with bearer-token auth, accepting `Users`
and `Groups`. Endpoint URL and bearer token are provided in your Ory
directory-sync setup-link.
